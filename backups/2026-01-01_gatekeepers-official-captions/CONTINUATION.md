   - **Example:** I wrote "secure smartphone" vs. official "General Dynamics Sectera Edge SME-PED, NSA Type 1 certified for TOP SECRET/SCI communications"
   - **Lesson:** Historical accuracy requires precise technical specifications, not general descriptions

   - **Eisenhower car phone:** 47 pounds, $1,400 (70% of Chevrolet price), VHF frequencies, 24-volt battery

### Domain Knowledge (Presidential History):

1. **Security vs. Convenience Tension:**
   - **Recurring theme:** Every president faces the same trade-off between security and usability
   - **Obama:** Fought 2 months to keep connectivity, got "twelve-ounce reminder that being President meant being alone"
   - **Trump:** Rejected secure devices as "too slow," used consumer iPhone despite security nightmare
   - **Biden:** Peloton modified for $12,000, used only 3 times because modifications made it useless

2. **Technology and Presidential Isolation:**
   - **Paradox:** More advanced communications technology increases presidential isolation
   - **Air Force One:** "Most sophisticated communication platform in human history, and the President was still isolated"
   - **Evolution:** Eisenhower could escape to golf, modern presidents can never escape

3. **Chiefs of Staff and Technology Discipline:**
   - **Baker (Reagan):** "Secure calls from the STU-III, everything else face-to-face, no exceptions"
   - **Lesson:** "The tool matters less than the discipline around its use"
   - **Haldeman (Nixon):** Built recording system so thorough it couldn't protect even him

### Best Practices (Caption Writing):

1. **Structure:**
   - **Opening:** Title with year/era
   - **Body:** Technical specifications + narrative story
   - **Closing:** Thematic observation connecting to larger book themes

2. **Technical Detail:**
   - Include exact weights, costs, model numbers
   - Specify security features (encryption, RF attenuation, physical modifications)
   - Correct common misreporting (e.g., Sectera Edge vs. BlackBerry)

3. **Narrative Elements:**
   - Include specific historical incidents (Suez Canal, Watergate gap, Panetta burritos)
   - Name specific people (John Foster Dulles, Dale Malechek, Leon Panetta)
   - Provide context that makes technical details meaningful

4. **Thematic Resonance:**
   - End with observation about power, isolation, burden, or presidential experience
   - Connect individual caption to book's larger narrative
   - Use memorable phrasing ("You don't win a technology war with the Commander-in-Chief")
