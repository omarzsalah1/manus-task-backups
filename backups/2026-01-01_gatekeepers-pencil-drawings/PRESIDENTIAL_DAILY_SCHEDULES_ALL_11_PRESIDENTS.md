## 1. LYNDON B. JOHNSON (1963-1969)
### "The Two-Shift Workaholic"

**Oval Office Time: 75-80% (HIGHEST)**

#### Representative Daily Schedule

**SHIFT ONE: 6:30 AM - 2:00 PM**

**6:30-7:00 AM** - Wake and morning news
- Wakes at 6:30 AM sharp
- Reads newspapers in bed: Washington Post, New York Times, Wall Street Journal
- Watches morning TV news (all three networks)
- Breakfast in bed: grapefruit, toast, coffee

**7:00-7:30 AM** - Morning briefing and phone calls
- Receives overnight intelligence briefing in bedroom
- Makes first phone calls of the day to staff
- Reviews schedule with appointments secretary
- Shaves and dresses while talking on phone

**7:30-8:00 AM** - Walk to Oval Office
- Arrives at Oval Office by 7:30 AM
- Begins reading overnight cables and memos
- Staff already waiting with urgent matters

**8:00 AM-12:00 PM** - Morning work session (Oval Office)
- Non-stop meetings with Cabinet, staff, lawmakers
- Phone calls to senators and congressmen
- Signs legislation and executive orders
- Reviews budget documents
- Dictates memos and letters
- "Treatment" sessions: arm-twisting lawmakers
- Paces while talking, invades personal space
- Famous for pulling people close by lapels

**12:00-12:30 PM** - Working lunch
- Lunch at desk or in small dining room
- Usually with key advisers or lawmakers
- Continues meetings while eating
- Favorite: chili, tapioca pudding

**12:30-2:00 PM** - Afternoon meetings and briefings
- More meetings with staff and Cabinet
- National Security Council briefings
- Congressional leadership meetings
- Press briefings and photo opportunities

**2:00-4:00 PM** - NAP TIME (mandatory)
- Returns to residence for nap
- Changes into pajamas
- Sleeps for 1-2 hours
- Staff knows not to disturb unless emergency

**SHIFT TWO: 4:00 PM - 2:00 AM**

**4:00-4:30 PM** - Wake from nap
- Wakes refreshed and energized
- Showers and changes into fresh suit
- Reviews afternoon schedule
- Returns to Oval Office

**4:30-7:00 PM** - Evening work session (Oval Office)
- Resumes meetings with fresh energy
- More phone calls to lawmakers
- Reviews legislation and policy proposals
- Meets with advisers on Vietnam War
- Signs more documents and memos

**7:00-8:00 PM** - Working dinner
- Dinner in residence or small dining room
- Usually with Lady Bird and close advisers
- Continues discussing business over meal
- Watches evening news on three TVs simultaneously

**8:00-11:00 PM** - Late evening work (Oval Office)
- Returns to Oval Office for more work
- Phone calls to lawmakers at home
- Reviews intelligence reports
- Dictates memos and letters
- Meets with staff on urgent matters
- "Night owl" sessions with key advisers

**11:00 PM-1:00 AM** - Late night phone calls and reading
- Moves to residence but continues working
- Makes phone calls from bedroom
- Reads briefing books and memos in bed
- Watches late night news
- Drinks Scotch and soda

**1:00-2:00 AM** - Final calls and bedtime
- Last phone calls of the day
- Reviews next day's schedule
- Finally goes to sleep around 2:00 AM
- Only 4-5 hours of sleep before 6:30 AM wake-up

#### Working Style Characteristics

**Location Distribution:**
- Oval Office: 75-80% of waking hours
- Residence: 15-20% (but still working)
- Other locations: 5%

**Key Traits:**
- Two-shift day with mandatory nap
- Non-stop meetings and phone calls
- Physical intimidation ("The Treatment")
- Three TVs watching all networks simultaneously
- Paced while talking, couldn't sit still
- Invaded personal space, pulled people close
- Called lawmakers at all hours (even at home)
- Dictated memos constantly
- Demanded instant responses
- "I want it now!" (famous phrase)

**Staff Impact:**
- Exhausted staff with brutal hours
- Expected staff to match his schedule
- Called staff at home late at night
- Demanded loyalty and availability 24/7
- High turnover due to burnout

## 2. RICHARD NIXON (1969-1974)
### "The Solitary Thinker"

**Oval Office Time: 35-40% (LOWEST until Trump)**

#### Representative Daily Schedule

**8:00-9:00 AM** - Wake and morning routine
- Wakes around 8:00 AM
- Breakfast alone in residence
- Reads newspapers: Washington Post, New York Times
- Minimal conversation with family
- Prefers solitude in morning

**9:00-9:30 AM** - Walk to Oval Office
- Arrives at Oval Office around 9:00 AM
- Reviews overnight memos and cables
- Meets briefly with Chief of Staff H.R. Haldeman
- Receives daily schedule and priorities

**9:30 AM-12:00 PM** - Morning meetings (Oval Office)
- National Security briefing with Kissinger
- Cabinet meetings (when scheduled)
- Meetings with Haldeman and Ehrlichman
- Congressional leadership meetings
- Photo opportunities and ceremonial events
- Prefers one-on-one meetings to groups

**12:00-1:00 PM** - Lunch alone
- Eats lunch alone in small dining room
- Cottage cheese with ketchup (favorite meal)
- Reads memos and briefing books while eating
- No staff or advisers present
- Values solitary time

**1:00-3:00 PM** - Afternoon meetings (Oval Office)
- More meetings with staff and Cabinet
- Phone calls to lawmakers
- Signs legislation and executive orders
- Press briefings (infrequent)
- Dictates memos to Haldeman

**3:00-6:00 PM** - Solitary work time (Lincoln Sitting Room)
- Leaves Oval Office for Lincoln Sitting Room
- Works alone on yellow legal pads
- Writes speeches and policy memos
- Makes notes on foreign policy
- No interruptions allowed
- Fire burning in fireplace
- Classical music playing

**6:00-7:00 PM** - Dinner
- Dinner alone or with Pat Nixon
- Minimal conversation
- Continues reading briefing books
- Watches evening news

**7:00-9:00 PM** - Evening work (Lincoln Sitting Room)
- Returns to Lincoln Sitting Room
- More solitary work on legal pads
- Reviews intelligence reports
- Makes notes on Vietnam strategy
- Listens to classical music
- Fire burning in fireplace

**9:00 PM-2:00 AM** - Late night solitary work
- Continues working alone in Lincoln Sitting Room
- Writes speeches and memos on yellow legal pads
- Makes phone calls to Haldeman and Kissinger
- Drinks Scotch (sometimes heavily)
- Listens to classical music
- Broods over enemies list
- Finally goes to bed around 2:00 AM

#### Working Style Characteristics

**Location Distribution:**
- Oval Office: 35-40% of waking hours
- Lincoln Sitting Room: 40-45% (solitary work)
- Residence: 15-20%
- Other locations: 5%

**Key Traits:**
- Extreme introvert, preferred solitude
- Worked alone on yellow legal pads
- Lincoln Sitting Room was true office
- Fire and classical music while working
- Cottage cheese with ketchup (odd favorite)
- Minimal interaction with staff
- One-on-one meetings preferred over groups
- Brooded over enemies and perceived slights
- Late night solitary work sessions
- Drank Scotch while working alone

**Staff Impact:**
- Haldeman and Ehrlichman controlled access
- Staff rarely saw president directly
- Communicated through memos, not meetings
- Haldeman was gatekeeper and enforcer
- Staff felt isolated from president

- Returns to pool for another swim

- Resumes meetings with fresh energy
- More phone calls to lawmakers

**10:00 PM** - Bedtime
- Goes to bed around 10:00 PM

#### Working Style Characteristics

**Location Distribution:**
- Oval Office: 65-70% of waking hours
- Pool: 5-10% (exercise breaks)
- Residence: 20-25%
- Other locations: 5%

**Key Traits:**
- Open-door policy for staff
- Accessible and collaborative
- Former congressman, loved working with Congress
- Two swim sessions daily (morning and afternoon)
- Listened more than talked
- Friendly and warm demeanor
- No imperial presidency style
- Smoked pipe while working
- Valued exercise and physical fitness
- Early to bed, early to rise

**Staff Impact:**
- Staff felt comfortable approaching him
- Collaborative decision-making
- Respected staff opinions
- Low turnover, high morale
- "Nice guy" reputation

## 4. JIMMY CARTER (1977-1981)
### "The Micromanager"

**Oval Office Time: 65-70%**

#### Representative Daily Schedule

**6:00-6:30 AM** - Wake and morning routine
- Wakes at 6:00 AM sharp
- Reads newspapers: Washington Post, New York Times, Atlanta Constitution
- Breakfast: bran cereal, fruit, juice
- Reviews overnight cables

**6:30-7:00 AM** - Morning jog
- Jogs around White House grounds
- 20-30 minutes of exercise
- Clears head before workday
- Showers and dresses

**7:00-7:30 AM** - Breakfast with Rosalynn
- Breakfast with Rosalynn Carter
- Discusses schedule and priorities
- Reviews family matters
- Prepares for day

**7:30-8:00 AM** - Walk to Oval Office
- Arrives at Oval Office by 7:30 AM (early!)
- Greets staff
- Reviews schedule with Chief of Staff
- Checks overnight developments

**8:00-9:00 AM** - Morning reading and memo review
- Reads every memo in detail
- Makes notes in margins
- Corrects grammar and spelling
- Micromanages details
- Staff memos often returned with corrections

**9:00-9:30 AM** - Intelligence briefing
- Daily intelligence briefing
- National Security briefing
- Asks detailed questions
- Takes extensive notes

**9:30 AM-12:00 PM** - Morning meetings (Oval Office)
- Cabinet meetings (reads every agenda item)
- Congressional leadership meetings
- Meetings with staff and advisers
- Reviews policy proposals in detail
- Questions every assumption
- Dives into minutiae

**12:00-12:30 PM** - Working lunch
- Lunch at desk in Oval Office
- Continues reading memos while eating
- Often eats alone to maximize work time
- Favorite: peanut butter sandwich

**12:30-3:00 PM** - Afternoon meetings (Oval Office)
- More meetings with Cabinet and staff
- Ceremonial events (minimal)
- Press briefings and interviews
- Signs legislation (after reading every page)
- Reviews budget line by line

**3:00-6:00 PM** - Late afternoon work (Oval Office)
- Continues reading memos and documents
- Makes phone calls to lawmakers
- Reviews policy proposals
- Meets with advisers on energy policy
- Micromanages details of every program

**6:00-6:30 PM** - Return to residence
- Walks back to residence
- Carries stack of briefing books
- Dinner with Rosalynn and Amy

**6:30-8:00 PM** - Dinner and family time
- Family dinner (important to Carter)
- Discusses day with Rosalynn
- Helps Amy with homework
- Watches evening news

**8:00 PM-12:00 AM** - Evening reading marathon
- Returns to study in residence
- Reads briefing books for hours
- Makes extensive notes
- Reviews legislation page by page
- Reads policy memos in detail
- Corrects grammar and spelling errors
- Micromanages every detail
- Works until midnight or later

**12:00 AM** - Bedtime
- Finally goes to bed around midnight
- Only 6 hours of sleep
- Wakes at 6:00 AM to repeat

#### Working Style Characteristics

**Location Distribution:**
- Oval Office: 65-70% of waking hours
- Residence study: 20-25% (evening reading)
- Other locations: 5-10%

**Key Traits:**
- Ultimate micromanager
- Read every memo in detail
- Corrected grammar and spelling
- Questioned every assumption
- Dived into minutiae
- Engineer's mindset: details matter
- Read legislation page by page
- Reviewed budget line by line
- Made extensive notes on everything
- Early riser (6:00 AM start)
- Late night reading marathons
- Jogged daily for exercise

**Staff Impact:**
- Staff felt micromanaged
- Memos returned with corrections
- Detailed questions on everything
- High expectations for accuracy
- Frustrated staff with detail obsession
- "He wants to know everything!"

#### Representative Daily Schedule

- Wakes around 7:00 AM

- Reviews schedule for day

- Tells jokes to staff
- Checks in with secretary

**8:45-9:00 AM** - Morning briefing prep
- Reviews schedule with Chief of Staff
- Receives overview of day's priorities
- Reads briefing cards (not long memos)
- Prepares for intelligence briefing

**9:00-9:30 AM** - Intelligence briefing
- Daily intelligence briefing (condensed version)
- National Security briefing
- Prefers oral briefings to reading
- Asks few questions, trusts advisers

**9:30 AM-12:00 PM** - Morning meetings (Oval Office)
- Cabinet meetings (delegates details to Cabinet)
- Congressional leadership meetings
- Meetings with staff and advisers
- Photo opportunities and ceremonial events
- Tells jokes and stories
- Delegates details to staff

**12:00-1:00 PM** - Lunch
- Lunch in Oval Office or small dining room

**3:00-5:00 PM** - Late afternoon work (Oval Office)

- Phone calls to lawmakers (when necessary)

**5:00-5:30 PM** - End of workday

- Exercises in White House gym
- Watches TV with Nancy
- Relaxes before dinner

**6:30-7:30 PM** - Dinner with Nancy
- Dinner with Nancy Reagan in residence
- Watches evening news together
- Discusses day (but not policy details)

**7:30-9:00 PM** - Evening relaxation
- Watches TV (old movies, westerns)
- Reads letters from Americans
- Responds to some letters personally
- Relaxes with Nancy

**9:00-10:00 PM** - Bedtime prep
- Reads briefing book for next day (briefly)
- Reviews schedule for tomorrow
- Watches late night news
- Prepares for bed

**10:00 PM** - Bedtime
- Goes to bed around 10:00 PM
- Gets 8-9 hours of sleep
- Values rest and recovery

#### Working Style Characteristics

**Location Distribution:**
- Oval Office: 50-55% of waking hours
- Residence: 40-45% (evenings off)
- Other locations: 5%

**Key Traits:**
- "9-to-5" schedule (unprecedented)
- Rarely worked evenings
- Delegated details to staff
- Preferred oral briefings to reading
- Told jokes and stories constantly
- Former actor, great communicator
- Trusted advisers to handle details
- Focused on big picture, not minutiae
- Valued rest and recovery
- Bowl of jelly beans on desk
- Watched old movies in evenings

**Staff Impact:**
- Staff handled details, Reagan handled vision
- Delegated extensively
- Trusted Cabinet to run departments
- "Let Reagan be Reagan" philosophy
- Low stress environment
- Staff appreciated work-life balance

## Conclusion: What the Schedules Reveal

The daily schedules of these eleven presidents reveal profound differences in leadership style, work habits, and personal priorities. From LBJ's two-shift workaholic marathon to Trump's "Executive Time" TV watching, from Carter's micromanaging detail obsession to Reagan's 9-to-5 delegation, each president brought a unique approach to the most demanding job in the world.

The **"% of time physically in Oval Office"** KPI tells a fascinating story:

- **LBJ (75-80%)** essentially lived in the Oval Office, working two shifts with a nap in between
- **Carter (65-70%)** and **Ford (65-70%)** spent most of their waking hours in the Oval Office
- **Bush 41 (60-65%)**, **Obama (55-60%)**, and **Biden (55-60%)** maintained strong Oval Office presence
- **Reagan (50-55%)**, **Clinton (50-55%)**, and **Bush 43 (50-55%)** balanced Oval Office time with other work locations
- **Nixon (35-40%)** preferred the Lincoln Sitting Room for solitary work
- **Trump (20-30%)** spent the least time in the Oval Office, preferring TV watching and phone calls

These schedules also reveal the evolution of the presidency itself. Earlier presidents like LBJ could work brutal hours with minimal public scrutiny. Later presidents faced 24/7 media coverage, social media pressures, and changing expectations about work-life balance. Trump's "Executive Time" and Biden's "Amtrak commuter" mentality represent opposite extremes of modern presidential working styles.

What remains constant across all eleven presidents is the crushing weight of responsibility, the relentless pace of decision-making, and the impossible task of balancing the demands of the office with personal health, family relationships, and basic human needs. Each president found their own way to navigate these challenges, with varying degrees of success.

The Chiefs of Staff who managed these diverse presidential working styles deserve recognition for their adaptability, patience, and skill in making the impossible work.
