## DRAWING 1: Reagan Recovery

### Image Information
- **File:** `Reagan_Recovery_Pencil_Drawing.png`
- **Original Source:** Catalog #C1538-12 (Reagan Presidential Library)
- **Historical Date:** March 1981 (4 days after assassination attempt)
- **Style:** Professional ethereal pencil drawing in Paul Kidby style

### Caption (Final, Ready for Typesetting)

**President Reagan works in his residence study, four days after returning to the White House following the March 1981 assassination attempt. The schedule resumed; the body required more time. (Ronald Reagan Presidential Library)**

### Book Placement
- **Chapter:** Chapter 6 or Chapter 8
- **Context:** Near discussion of presidential incapacity/recovery
- **Page Layout:** Full-page or half-page illustration

### Historical Significance
This drawing captures a pivotal moment in presidential history—the continuity of government during crisis. President Reagan, still recovering from John Hinckley Jr.'s assassination attempt, meets with Chief of Staff James Baker III and Deputy Chief of Staff Michael Deaver in his White House residence study. Reagan's casual attire (cardigan sweater and white pants) contrasts with Baker and Deaver's business suits, emphasizing the vulnerability of the moment while demonstrating the resilience of presidential leadership. The caption's poignant observation—"The schedule resumed; the body required more time"—captures the tension between duty and recovery that defines presidential crisis management.

## DRAWING 2: Biden Presidential Daily Brief

### Image Information
- **File:** `Biden_PDB_Pencil_Drawing.png`
- **Original Source:** Photo ID P20210122AS-0210 (White House Flickr)
- **Historical Date:** January 22, 2021 (Biden's first full day in office)
- **Style:** Professional ethereal pencil drawing in Paul Kidby style

### Caption (Final, Ready for Typesetting)

**President Biden receives the Presidential Daily Brief with Vice President Harris in the Oval Office, January 22, 2021—his first full day in office. The Restoration Era began with a return to structured morning intelligence briefings, reversing the Anomaly Era's abbreviated and irregular approach. (Official White House Photo)**

### Book Placement
- **Chapter:** Chapter 10 or Conclusion
- **Context:** Near "9 AM Protocol" discussion
- **Page Layout:** Full-page or half-page illustration

### Historical Significance
This drawing represents the beginning of what the book terms the "Restoration Era"—the return to traditional presidential scheduling protocols after the "Anomaly Era" of the Trump presidency. President Biden and Vice President Kamala Harris (the first female, first Black, and first South Asian Vice President) receive the Presidential Daily Brief in the Oval Office, surrounded by staff members. The formal Oval Office setting, with presidential portraits visible on the walls and a fire burning in the fireplace (Biden's preference), symbolizes the restoration of structured morning intelligence briefings. Some staff members wear COVID masks, marking this as a historical document of the pandemic era. The scene contrasts sharply with the previous administration's abbreviated and irregular approach to intelligence briefings, embodying the book's central thesis about the critical role of Chiefs of Staff in maintaining presidential discipline and schedule structure.
