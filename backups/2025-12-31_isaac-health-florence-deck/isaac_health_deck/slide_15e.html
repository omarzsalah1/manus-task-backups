Escalation Protocols: Clinical & Behavioral Triggers

Florence Knows When to Escalate—And What Type of Response Is Needed

+
Clinical Triggers
Vital Sign Abnormality
Temp >101°F, BP >180/110, HR >120 or <50
Pain Escalation
Pain score ≥7/10 or sudden onset
Suicidal Ideation
Any mention → immediate RN transfer
Medication Error
Missed doses, wrong medication, adverse reaction
Fall or Injury
Any reported fall, head injury, or fracture
!
Behavioral Triggers
Wandering / Exit-Seeking
Leaving home, found outside, attempting to leave
Aggressive Behavior
Verbal threats, physical aggression, property damage
Unsafe Environment
Stove left on, doors unlocked, caregiver absent
Acute Confusion
Sudden disorientation, hallucinations, paranoia
Caregiver Distress
Caregiver overwhelmed, unable to manage patient
All Escalations Trigger Immediate Response:
1
RN Alert in <60 sec
2
Caregiver Notified
3
Care Navigator Briefed
